Case Study: 
UPI "Collect Request" Fraud
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves cybercriminals manipulating users into authorizing money transfers under false pretenses. 
A highly prevalent variation is the "Collect Request" scam, which exploits a user's misunderstanding of how UPI PINs function.

How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step):

1) The Setup:
A victim lists a secondhand item for sale on an online marketplace (e.g., OLX, Facebook Marketplace).

2) The Hook: 
A scammer poses as an eager buyer, agreeing to the asking price immediately without negotiation and offering to pay upfront via a UPI app.

3) The Trap: 
Instead of sending money, the scammer initiates a UPI "Collect Request" (a request for money) to the victim's phone number.

4) The Deception: 
The scammer calls the victim, claiming the payment requires authorization. 
They instruct the victim to open their UPI app, click "Pay" or "Accept" on the pending notification, and enter their UPI PIN, falsely claiming this step is required to receive the funds.

5) The Theft: 
Believing they are authorizing an inbound payment, the victim enters their secret PIN. 
Instantly, the requested amount is deducted from the victim's account and transferred to the scammer.


Targets and Consequences:
This crime primarily targets peer-to-peer online sellers, particularly those who are less tech-savvy and unaware that a UPI PIN is only required to send money, never to receive it. 
The immediate consequence is a direct, irreversible financial loss. 
Victims also frequently experience severe emotional distress and a lasting loss of confidence in digital financial ecosystems.

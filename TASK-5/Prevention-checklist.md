Prevention Checklist 
Stay Safe Online: A Cybersecurity Checklist for College Students

# General Security & Privacy
1) Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):
   Turn on 2FA for all critical accounts, including your university email, social media, and banking apps.

2) Beware of Free Campus/Cafe Wi-Fi:
   Avoid accessing banking portals or entering sensitive passwords while connected to public, unsecured networks. Use a trusted VPN if necessary.

3) Verify "Too Good to Be True" Opportunities:
   Be highly skeptical of unsolicited WhatsApp messages or emails offering high-paying part-time jobs or internships that require an upfront "registration fee."

4) Lock Down Social Media: Set your profiles to private.
   Avoid oversharing live locations or daily routines that could be exploited for stalking or social engineering.

5) Update Your Devices:
    Regularly update your phone, laptop OS, and apps to ensure you have the latest security patches.

6) Identify KYC Scams: Never click on links in SMS messages claiming your bank account or SIM card will be blocked pending an urgent "KYC update."
    Always contact the bank directly through official numbers.

# Financial & UPI Safety
1) Remember the Golden UPI Rule: 
You will never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. 
Your PIN is strictly for sending funds or checking your balance.

2) Scrutinize "Collect Requests": 
If you receive an unexpected notification on your UPI app asking you to approve a payment, decline it immediately unless you personally initiated the transaction.

# Reporting Cybercrime in India
If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime or online fraud, report it immediately.
